### WaffleStudio Toy Project
Dec 2022 - Feb 2023 \
*Web & App Development Project hosted by Seoul National University*

<details>
<p><b>Project Description</b>
WaffleStudio is one of the largest development clubs in Seoul National University. Annually, the club's leadership arranges a toy project session where participants engage in an extensive development process. This involves initial team formation, project ideation, followed by the execution and deployment of the service for public access. At the end of the session, teams are evaluated and prizes are awarded to those achieving top ranks.</p>

<p><b>Contributions</b>
Our team developed a sharable calendar web service using Next.js and Django. Through this platform, users can efficiently organize their schedules, create posts linked to specific events, and share these schedules to get comments from others. I played a role as a frontend developer. Our team secured the third position among all the competing participants.</p>

### K-Startup Maker Project
Jul 2020 - Dec 2020 \
*Maker Project hosted by the Korean Government*

<details>
<p><b>Project Description</b>
The term 'maker' refers to creators who develop products through various tools and creative ideas. The Maker Project, aimed at supporting such makers, is organized by the Ministry of SMEs and Startups of the South Korean government, providing 5,000$ for selected projects to complete the process of product development.</p>

<p><b>Contributions</b>
Our team suggested "Sensorless Robot Navigation via External Camera Monitoring" to address the issue of the expensive production cost of robots equipped with sensors. Subsequently, we were awarded 5,000 dollars to carry out the project. In this project, we developed software for indoor autonomous robots, employing U-Net for driving area detection and DOPE (Deep Object Pose Estimation) for robot position detection. As vision team leader, I managed the entire process, including data collection, labeling, training, and inference.</p>

<p><b>Code</b>
<a href="https://github.com/snuzero2020/zero_maker">github</a>
</p>

</details>

### International Student Car Competition 2020
Apr 2020 - Aug 2020 \
*Electric Car Competition for Students at an International Level, Autonomous Car Category*

<details>
<p><b>Project Description</b>
Organized by the Ministry of Land, Infrastructure, and Transport of South Korea, this undergraduate competition assesses teams based on their capacity to develop an autonomous driving platform and software. Throughout the event, the platform is required to manage diverse autonomous driving challenges, including tasks such as recognizing traffic light colors, parking, and navigating through various obstacles, all with the aim of reaching the finish line quickly.</p>

<p><b>Contributions</b>
Our team was composed of three specialized units: Vision, SLAM, and Path Planning. Collaboratively, these units worked to create a comprehensive autonomous driving structure, encompassing both the platform and the software. As a member of the vision team, I actively engaged in developing real-time parking lot detection by modifying YOLOv3's structure and loss function and implementing accurate lane detection using LaneNet.</p>
